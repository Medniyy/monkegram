# Signal Jungle

Signal Jungle treats digital identity as a living broadcast: a strict frame
holding an unruly, expressive presence. Space is deep and nocturnal, with a
single luminous signal cutting through it. Forms are bold, geometric, and
immediately legible, while their edges carry small signs of analog transmission.
Every alignment must feel meticulously crafted and deliberately tuned.

Color does the explanatory work. Dark monke green creates depth; banana gold
marks agency, motion, and the moment of capture. Cream appears sparingly for
human warmth, and functional red exists only as an exact recording cue. The
palette should feel painstakingly calibrated by someone at the top of their
field, never decorative or accidental.

Scale alternates between emblematic and intimate. The mark can become
monumental, while tiny system labels and frame indices create rhythm around it.
Repeated squares, capture brackets, and segmented timing marks suggest a
machine studying personality in real time. The result should reward close
inspection and appear to be the product of countless hours of expert refinement.

Composition is asymmetric but balanced: one dominant visual event, one clear
path for the eye, and disciplined negative space. Images should feel observed
through a camera system rather than placed into a template. Text remains rare
and essential—short commands, identifiers, and signal-like phrases integrated
into the visual architecture.

Craft is the movement’s proof. Pixel edges must be crisp, spacing exact, and
every repeated mark consistent. The final artifact should look labored over with
master-level attention, with no generic decoration and no unresolved overlap.
Its niche reference is a monkey returning through a camera signal: recognizable
to insiders, but experienced by everyone else as a confident, mysterious visual
system.
